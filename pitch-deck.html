02 · The Problem & Science
Generic books. A missed opportunity
that psychology explains.

Every night, parents read their children books about someone else's family, with no developmental intentionality. The science says this is a missed opportunity nobody has fixed.

Research-backed
Children encode personally relevant stories in identity and long-term memory regions, not generic content. This is established developmental science that no consumer product has applied at scale.
Fivush · Emory

Elaborative reminiscing shapes a child's autobiographical memory, identity, and resilience. Advisory formalisation in progress.

Komisar

Physical, emotionally resonant objects reinforce secure parent-child attachment in the critical early years.

Siegel · UCLA

Shared storytelling integrates both brain hemispheres, building emotional regulation and resilience.
